In Context

Influence of Music Concrete

Musicians of the past and present have been experimenting with the world around us as an instrument which constructs their musical visions. Composers such as Aube(Akifumi Nakajima), Matthew Herbert and Pierre Schaeffer have recorded and produced albums using solely found natural sounds. The sound of water, the hum of fluorescent lamps, human voices, heartbeats, pages being ripped from the bible, Tesco products, audio recordings from the life, death and consumption of a pig, these have all become concept albums which explore music outside the normal conventions of what is thought in music school. I would argue that it is not the breaking of rules these composers are intent on displaying, but rather a view of reassessing the order in which we hear sounds and how they are displayed. R.Murray Schaffer writes in his book "The soundscape: Our Sonic Environment and The Tuning of the World":

"Today all sounds belong to a continuous field of possibilities lying within the comprehensive dominion of music. Behold the new orchestra: the sonic universe! And the musicians: anyone and anything that sounds!"

Schaffer statement sums up the ideology behind the movement of Music Concrete composers both past and present, and when coupled with Thomas Edison's invention of the phonograph, ones experience can now be recorded and shared with the wider world. Contact mics have been used not only in musical compositions but also by Foley artists recording audio for tv and film. The sound of Ants walking can easily be recorded and added to a video soundtrack, a door closing, a clock ticking, the possibilities are endless.

Good Vibrations

Contact mics work on the principle of picking up frequencies from vibrating surfaces, they differ with this regard from the conventional microphone which picks up vibrations of molecules in the air. An advantage of having a microphone with this property is that background and unwanted sound is filtered out and the surface, any surface can become the composers amplified instrument or recording source. Voice can be recorded, but only through the vibration of a material i.e. by pressing the contact mic against the throat or by singing into a container with contact mic attached to the container. Similarly any acoustic instrument such as a guitar or viloin can be amplified by adding the piezo disc to the body of the instrument. But why buy instruments, musician and artist Ken Butler's alternative is the creation of his own instruments from found objects. By following the principle of the Diddly bow attaching stings to the body of an object and amplifying it anything can become the instrument.
